There is a new kind of performance we want to include in our project: the Lecture-Demonstration aka lecdem. 

A lecdem is how a musician transmits knowledge of their bani through the genealogy tree of music. They are also profoundly important for the listening experience as they are clear articulations of intent of the music and the aesthetics of the art. They enhance the experience of rasikas by revealing the depth of the ocean of Music without which we're only left to guess: now we can have it acknowledged by the musicians themselves.

All reputed Carnatic Musicians present lecdems and rasikas eagerly listen to them. We need them in our system.

Lecdems can be about musicians (and composers by extension), compositions, ragas, and about Carnatic music itself.

We want to add the following:

1) A specifier to a youtube video that labels it as a lecdem.
2) The lecdem should show as its own category in the Musician panel (alongside Recordings and Compositions). 
3) Lecdems deserve their own chip style, like all other first class citizens of our system: like raga, composition, musician, composer. A lecdem is an important first class object in our system.
4) Lecdems are by Musicians so natually appear in the Musicians tab. But really Lecdems have two flavors: Lecdems-by are by the Musicians; Lecdems-about are about ragas, compositions, and musicians. So every Musician will not only have a section that shows the lecdems by them, but also _about_ them. For example, we can have lecdem recordings by MD Ramanathan, but we also show lecdem recordings _about_ him which are by other musicians (ONLY musicians own lecdems: else they cannot exist in our system).
5) Lecdems are also about ragas and compositions so these should show up as a separate section in the Bani flow tab. For example, we can have a lecdem on the commonality of Surutti, Kedaragowla and Narayana Gowla: this lecdem should show up when these ragas are looked up or appear in the Bani-Flow panel. This will give the rasika indications to listen and learn more about these ragas.
6) Lecdems can sometimes be about neither ragas, compositions, nor musicians. In such a case they will appear only in the Lecdems-by section under Musicians. For example, TM Krishna's lecdems on Manodharma belong in this category.
7) Lecdems should not be treated as general misc objects: they are separate and should be treated as first class citizens in our system.
8) When adding a youtube video via a form, the user should be able to flag it as a lecdem and provide details of which ragas, compositions, and musicians it talks about. Note the plural: it should be possible to assign a number of these. All items should be dropdownable to avoid duplication errors. If the items don't exist they can be manually added by the user. All this should be finally bundled into a json file and parsed by our clie tools so we can inject them(lecdems) appropriately into our final html file.
9) Lecdems cannot be looked up, they can only be discovered! This is the fun of it actually: users stumble upon them when they are searching for ragas, composition, and/or musicians. We want to preserve the excitement of discovering these. Not every object will have a lecdem: lecdems are rare but will keep the rasika searching to find more and to improve the depths of their understanding.
