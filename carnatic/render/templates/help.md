## About Bani Flow

Bani Flow maps the *guru-shishya* (teacher-student) lineage of Carnatic classical music —
how ragas, compositions, and performance style pass between generations of musicians.
Explore how different artists have rendered the same raga or composition to hear the living
variety within a single tradition.

## Guru-Shishya View

A directed graph of musicians. Edges flow guru → shishya.
Tap a node once to see a brief preview; tap again to open the **Musician** panel.
The filter chips at the top narrow the graph by era (colour-coded by period) or
instrument (shape-coded by family).

## Mela-Janya View

A wheel of the 72 melakarta ragas with their janya (derived) ragas branching outward.
Tap a raga once to preview; tap again to open the **Bani Flow** panel, which lists all
recordings on that raga. Use <span class="raga-chip" style="pointer-events:none;">raga name</span> links to navigate across ragas.

## Musician Panel

Shows the repertoire of a selected musician — concert recordings grouped by performance,
each tagged with a <span class="comp-chip" style="pointer-events:none;">composition</span> and
<span class="raga-chip" style="pointer-events:none;">raga</span>.
Tap the ▶ button on any recording to open it in the YouTube player.

## Bani Flow Panel

Search for a raga or composition in the left sidebar to see every musician who has recorded it,
in order of recency. Compare how a guru and their students each render
<span class="raga-chip" style="pointer-events:none;">Thodi</span> or
<span class="raga-chip" style="pointer-events:none;">Bhairavi</span> — this is the heart of the exploration.

## YouTube Player

Opens in a floating window (desktop) or a slide-up strip (mobile).
Click the <span class="mp-artist-chip" style="pointer-events:none;font-size:0.70rem;">artist</span>
chip in the player bar to jump to that musician in the graph.
The tracklist button (☰) lets you switch tracks within a concert.

## E-Tanpura

The row of note buttons in the header plays an electronic tanpura drone for that *sruti* (tonic pitch).
Use it as a pitch reference while listening or singing along.
Click the lit button again — or the ● power indicator — to stop the drone.
